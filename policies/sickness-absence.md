# Sickness and Absence Policy

**Internal use only — not for external distribution**
*Last reviewed: January 2025 | Owner: People Operations*

---

## Reporting Absence

If you are too unwell to work, you must notify your manager (or their cover) **before your normal start time** on the first day of absence. Please phone rather than message where possible. You should keep your manager updated each day you are absent unless a longer period has been agreed.

---

## Self-Certification

For absences of **1–7 calendar days**, you self-certify on return using the form in Rippling.

---

## Fit Notes

For absences of **8 or more consecutive calendar days**, you must provide a fit note (Statement of Fitness for Work) from a GP or other registered healthcare professional. Fit notes should be submitted to People Operations promptly. Ongoing absences require updated fit notes covering the full period.

---

## Company Sick Pay

Paluza provides enhanced sick pay above the statutory minimum, subject to satisfactory completion of probation:

| Period of absence | Pay |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Full basic pay |
| Weeks 5–8 | Half basic pay |
| Beyond 8 weeks | Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) only |

SSP is currently £116.75 per week (2024/25 rate). Employees not eligible for SSP (e.g. earnings below the lower earnings limit) should contact People Operations.

Company sick pay entitlement resets each rolling 12-month period.

---

## Return to Work

On your first day back, your manager will have a brief, informal return-to-work conversation. This is supportive in nature — it's a chance to make sure you're well enough to be back and to discuss any adjustments needed. A short record is kept in Rippling.

---

## Absence Triggers

Frequent short-term absences may prompt a supportive review conversation. Trigger points are:

- **3 or more separate absences** in any rolling 12-month period, or
- **10 or more working days** absent in any rolling 12-month period

Triggering these points does not imply any wrongdoing — the purpose is to offer support and understand whether there is anything Paluza can do to help.

---

## Long-Term Absence

Where an employee has been absent continuously for **4 weeks or more**, People Operations will make contact to discuss next steps, which may include an occupational health referral, a phased return-to-work plan, or consideration of reasonable adjustments.

---

## Medical Appointments

Routine medical, dental, and optical appointments should be scheduled outside working hours where possible. Where this is not practical, short periods of time off (e.g. an hour for a GP appointment) are generally granted without deduction at manager discretion. Longer appointments or repeated time off should be discussed with your manager in advance.
