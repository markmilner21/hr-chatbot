# Probationary Period Policy

**Internal use only — not for external distribution**
*Last reviewed: January 2025 | Owner: People Operations*

---

## Overview

All new employees join Paluza subject to a probationary period. The purpose of probation is to allow both the employee and Paluza to assess whether the role is the right fit, and to give new joiners structured support as they settle in.

---

## Duration

| Role level | Probation length |
|---|---|
| Standard roles | 3 months |
| Management and senior individual contributor roles | 6 months |

The applicable probation length will be stated in your offer letter and contract.

---

## During Probation

Your manager is responsible for:
- Setting clear expectations for the probationary period
- Holding a **check-in at approximately 6 weeks** to discuss progress informally
- Conducting a **formal review** at the end of the probationary period

You are encouraged to raise any concerns or questions throughout — this is a two-way process.

---

## Completing Probation Successfully

Where the formal review confirms satisfactory performance, your manager will confirm in writing (via Rippling) that your probationary period has been completed successfully. This is also when any service-linked benefits that require probation completion take effect.

---

## Extension

If there are concerns about performance or conduct, or if the review has not been possible (e.g. due to extended absence), the probationary period may be extended. Extensions should not take the total probationary period beyond **6 months**. An extension must be confirmed in writing and should include clear objectives and a revised review date.

---

## Notice During Probation

During the probationary period, the notice required by either party is **1 week**. This applies whether the employee resigns or Paluza terminates the employment.

---

## Termination During Probation

Paluza may terminate employment during probation where performance or conduct is not meeting the required standard. This will normally follow a conversation with the employee and involve People Operations. Statutory rights (including the right not to be dismissed for automatically unfair reasons) apply from day one of employment regardless of probationary status.
