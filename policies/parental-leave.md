# Parental Leave Policy

**Internal use only — not for external distribution**
*Last reviewed: January 2025 | Owner: People Operations*

---

## Maternity Leave

All employees are entitled to up to **52 weeks** of maternity leave (26 weeks Ordinary Maternity Leave + 26 weeks Additional Maternity Leave), regardless of length of service.

**Enhanced maternity pay** (subject to completing probation and 6 months' service by the 15th week before the expected week of childbirth):

| Period | Pay |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–16 | Full basic pay |
| Weeks 17–39 | Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP): £184.03/week or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower |
| Weeks 40–52 | Unpaid |

Employees who do not qualify for enhanced pay may still qualify for SMP or Maternity Allowance — People Operations can advise.

**Notice:** You must notify People Operations of your intention to take maternity leave by the **15th week before your expected week of childbirth** (your MATB1 form will be needed).

---

## Paternity Leave

Eligible employees may take **1 or 2 consecutive weeks** of paternity leave within 56 days of the birth or placement.

Paluza enhances paternity pay to **full basic pay** for the duration (statutory rate is £184.03/week or 90% of average weekly earnings).

To be eligible, you must have at least 26 weeks' continuous service by the 15th week before the expected week of childbirth.

---

## Shared Parental Leave (SPL)

Where the mother/primary adopter curtails their maternity or adoption leave, remaining leave and pay can be shared between eligible partners as Shared Parental Leave.

Paluza's SPL pay mirrors the enhanced maternity pay above (where the individual meets the service and earnings eligibility requirements).

SPL requires advance notice and agreement on a leave schedule. People Operations can walk you through the process.

---

## Adoption Leave

The primary adopter is entitled to the same leave and enhanced pay as maternity leave above. The secondary adopter is entitled to paternity leave equivalent. Both must notify People Operations as soon as possible after the match is confirmed.

---

## Keeping in Touch (KIT) Days

Employees on maternity or adoption leave can work up to **10 KIT days** without bringing their leave to an end. These must be agreed in advance with your manager. KIT days are paid at your normal daily rate.

---

## Returning from Parental Leave

Employees returning from up to 26 weeks' leave have the right to return to the same job. Employees returning from more than 26 weeks' leave have the right to return to the same job or, if that is not reasonably practicable, a suitable alternative on equivalent terms.

If you are considering a change to your working pattern on return (e.g. reduced hours), this should be submitted as a flexible working request at least 8 weeks before your planned return.
