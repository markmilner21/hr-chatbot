# Annual Leave Policy

**Internal use only — not for external distribution**
*Last reviewed: January 2025 | Owner: People Operations*

---

## Entitlement

All full-time employees receive **25 days** of annual leave per year, plus 8 public holidays (England & Wales).

Entitlement increases with length of service:

| Completed years of service | Annual leave entitlement |
|---|---|
| 0–1 years | 25 days |
| 2–3 years | 26 days |
| 4+ years | 27 days |

Part-time employees receive a pro-rated entitlement based on their contracted hours.

Employees who join or leave part-way through the leave year (1 January–31 December) will have their entitlement pro-rated accordingly.

---

## Booking Leave

All annual leave must be booked in advance through **Rippling**. Leave is subject to manager approval and business need.

**Notice expectations:**
- Single days or short periods: as much notice as possible, at least 48 hours
- One week or more: at least twice the duration in advance (e.g. two weeks' notice for a one-week holiday)

Managers should respond to requests within 3 working days. Requests may be declined during peak periods or where cover cannot be arranged — this should be discussed with the employee so alternative dates can be found.

---

## Carrying Over Leave

Up to **5 days** of unused annual leave may be carried over into the following leave year. Carried-over leave must be used by **31 March** or it will be forfeited.

Exceptions (e.g. where leave could not be taken due to long-term sickness or family leave) should be discussed with People Operations.

---

## Buying and Selling Leave

Employees may buy or sell up to **5 days** of annual leave per scheme year (April–March) through the flexible benefits portal. Elections must be made during the annual window in March. Bought leave is deducted from salary in equal monthly instalments.

---

## Public Holidays

Paluza observes the 8 standard England & Wales public holidays. Employees in Scotland or Northern Ireland should speak to People Operations about the applicable dates for their location.

Employees required to work on a public holiday will receive a day in lieu to be taken at a mutually agreed time.

---

## Leave During Notice Periods

Holiday continues to accrue during a notice period. Employees may be asked to take any remaining leave during their notice period. Where this is not practical, outstanding leave will be paid out at termination.
