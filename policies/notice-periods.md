# Notice Periods Policy

**Internal use only — not for external distribution**
*Last reviewed: January 2025 | Owner: People Operations*

---

## Contractual Notice

Notice periods at Paluza vary by role and are set out in individual contracts of employment. The following are the **standard contractual notice periods** post-probation:

| Completed length of service | Notice (employee or employer) |
|---|---|
| During probation | 1 week (either side) |
| Up to 2 years | 1 month |
| 2–5 years | 6 weeks |
| 5–10 years | 3 months |
| 10+ years | 3 months |

Note: statutory minimum notice (1 week per completed year of service, up to 12 weeks) applies where it exceeds the contractual figure. Individual contracts for senior or specialist roles may specify longer notice periods — always refer to your contract.

---

## Giving Notice

Employees wishing to resign should submit notice in writing to their manager, with a copy to People Operations. Notice begins the day after it is received.

---

## Receiving Notice

Where Paluza terminates employment (other than for gross misconduct), written notice will be provided and the period above applies.

---

## Pay in Lieu of Notice (PILON)

Paluza reserves the right to make a payment in lieu of some or all of the notice period rather than requiring the employee to work it. This will be confirmed in writing.

---

## Garden Leave

During a notice period, Paluza may place an employee on garden leave — they remain employed and continue to receive pay and benefits but are not required (or permitted) to attend the workplace or carry out their duties. This is typically used where there is a risk to confidential information or customer relationships.

---

## Annual Leave During Notice

Holiday continues to accrue during any notice period. Employees may be asked to take outstanding leave during their notice. Any accrued but untaken leave will be paid out on termination. Taking leave not yet accrued during the notice period is not permitted without prior agreement.
